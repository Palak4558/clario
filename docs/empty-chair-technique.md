# Background: the Empty Chair technique

A quick primer for anyone working on this repo who isn't familiar with the
therapeutic technique the `empty-chair` feature is modeled on.

The Empty Chair technique comes out of Gestalt therapy (developed mid-20th
century). The core idea: a person sits across from an empty chair and
imagines someone (or some part of themselves) sitting in it — someone they
have unresolved feelings toward, a version of themselves, an inner critic,
etc. They speak to that imagined presence directly, then physically switch
seats and respond as if they were that other person/part, continuing the
back-and-forth. It's typically run with a therapist guiding the pacing and
helping the person process what comes up.

It's commonly used for grief, unresolved conflict, self-criticism, anxiety
about a future conversation, and generally any situation where saying
something out loud — even to an empty chair — helps someone get past being
stuck in their own head about it.

In this app, that real-world technique is adapted into a two-perspective
text dialogue ("BLUE Chair" = the user, "RED Chair" = the person/feeling
they're addressing), with an AI facilitator standing in for the therapist's
role of prompting reflection and nudging perspective switches — without
ever giving advice or interpreting the conversation for the user. See
[`empty-chair/README.md`](../empty-chair/README.md) for the technical
implementation.

This is a general, non-clinical summary written for this repo — it isn't
medical advice, and the app itself isn't a substitute for therapy.
